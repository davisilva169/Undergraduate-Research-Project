# Undergraduate Research Project: Energy Division in Cosmic Ray Cascades

Repository containing my undergraduate research project on cosmic ray cascades. This work investigates the leading particle effect by developing statistical models and tests for energy splitting processes, combining data analysis and computational methods.

You can read the complete abstract of the project [clicking here](Abstract.md).

## 🪐 About the Project
This research was developed at the Institute of Physics of São Carlos (IFSC) at University of São Paulo (USP). The primary goal is to determine well-defined statistical criteria to quantify the leading particle effect in the early stages of atmospheric cosmic ray showers. 

## 🔬 Methodology
* **Null Model:** Built a uniform fragmentation null model in Python to describe a purely random and uniform energy division.
* **Theoretical Refinement:** Applied the Law of Total Probability to the model to incorporate the variable multiplicity of particles.
* **Simulations:** Confronted the mathematical model with realistic Monte Carlo simulations generated by the CONEX software.
* **Data Scope:** Analyzed proton-initiated showers with energies from 10¹⁰ to 10²⁰ eV using the SYBILS hadronic interaction model.

## 🚀 Key Results
* Revealed a sharp discrepancy between the uniform null model and CONEX data, providing strong evidence for the leading particle effect.
* Discovered that the leading particles, which concentrate most of the primary energy, are predominantly pions.
* Established a direct and promising connection to the **Muon Puzzle**, as muon production depends directly on pion decay.
